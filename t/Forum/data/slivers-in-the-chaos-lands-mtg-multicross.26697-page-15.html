    To save everyone some energy:

    - Sliver mindspeech does not work with non-slivers aside from the very vague sense you've been getting. You'll either need to learn each language in turn or find some magic spell / item to do translation.

    - The mechanic for language learning is 3 turns to achieve full conversational level. An extra turn gets you complete fluency or literacy. So, 5 turns if you want to speak like a native and be literate, but you can choose which order to get fluency and literacy. You can't become literate until you have 3 points in the language.

    You don't need full fluency to find languages useful. The levels are:

    (1) Awkward: You need the other person to speak slowly and use short words. You can only exchange very simple ideas -- where is the X, I want food, etc.

    (2) Basic: You stumble a lot, don't understand nuance or idiom, and you sound like a bit of a simpleton, but you can hold conversation on most non-technical topics.

    (3) Conversational: You have a thick accent and idioms are a problem, but otherwise you can handle whatever you need to do, linguistically speaking.

    (4) Fluent: You speak like a native.

    This quest isn't just about combat, though. Utility powers are far more game-changing than fireballs. For example, if we assume that Shifting Sliver's power is, at a minimum:
    1. He can walk through walls while wearing clothes / carrying stuff.
    2. He needs to breathe.
    3. Gravity still affects him, but he can choose what is solid to him such that doesn't fall down to the center of the earth. (If gravity doesn't affect him then Shifting is strictly better than Winged.)

    In the Warhammer quest Shifting is actually quite a bit stronger than this, since he can give other slivers his ability and all Shifted slivers are immune to non-magical damage.

    Given the definition I've given, here are some exploits that break the game:

    1) [The obvious one.] Walk through enemy fortifications to assassinate their leaders / specials.

    2) Land submarines, as outlined here.

    3) Walk into an object while carrying a big rock. Let go of the rock and walk away. One of two things happens: the rock becomes solid while overlapped with another object, or it remains ethereal.
    1. If it remains ethereal then you have an unstealable, unfindable object that Shifting can come back and pick up at any time. This is a perfect way to pre-position caches of supplies. It's also perfect food preservation, since he can choose for the bacteria to remain solid while the food becomes ethereal.
    2. If it becomes solid, what happens? If it explodes then you have a trivial way to break walls and kill people. You also have a free energy source. If it just remains merged then you could grapple with enemies and embed them in the ground. Perfect way to farm trolls -- stick them so that their limbs and top of their head are in the wall. They can't move to attack you and they can't get purchase to escape. You can carve fillets off all day long, then let them regenerate.
    4) Strap on a lot of explosives, walk into the enemy ranks, and then let the explosives be non-ethereal. He's a reusable "suicide" bomber.


    These are things I came up with after thinking for less than ten seconds. You can quibble with specific bits if you want -- e.g. maybe he can't leave the bacteria behind, or maybe trolls are strong enough to pull their way out of rock -- but the point is that etherealness is utterly broken if you're allowed to fully exploit it. There's two ways to deal with this: fiat or exclusion. Aranfan chose to go with fiat ("no, you can't do X because I said so"). That's fine when the game that you want to play is more or less a turn-based 4X game, but that's not the game I want to play. I want this to be a rational world where you can apply your abilities and your creativity to the fullest. I also want it to last more than a couple dozen turns. As such, I need to keep Shifting out of the game. (Note that I'm not criticizing Aranfan's choice. He wants to play a different kind of game than I want to play, that's all.)

    Now, to be clear: if I let X into the game (for whatever X) and you then find an exploit that turns it into an instant win button -- good for you! I will totally roll with that and let you win. I just don't want to make it trivial.
