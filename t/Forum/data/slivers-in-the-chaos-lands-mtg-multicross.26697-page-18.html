It's unclear if this means just the wells won't shift (but everything around them will), or if the wells protect a larger portion of area (so a small well might cover a few acres in a forest for example), or if it's something else entirely.     As far as queen actions go: we probably want to hunt, we might want to mana-bond (especially if we're going for the constant replenish option), and we might want to clear trees (if we're big enough) to improve visibility (so we aren't ambushed right outside our home again), we might want to learn english, we might want to research a spell. This means we probably want to design our defenses accordingly (so they'll be great against the ogres, but not so good if another thing, like, the elves, decided to attack us, e.g. focus on deadly, less on hidden, and less on moral breaking).     Alright, before getting into the knuts and bolts of what to do now that we've got some sliver-power, lets make sure we've at least identified all the options. Here's what I see as our options (for mid-game goals)

    1. Fortify where we are. We want a base of operations, might as well set up here. We've got some ogres and snobish elves to deal with, but it's unlikely that we'll find somewhere with no residents which would be easy for us to move to.

    2. Nomads - the chaos lands are not kind to those in one spot (citation required), let's live as a nomadic tribe. At the least until we find a nice unpopulated area void of ogre attacks to settle down in - This seems like a bad idea, given the relative benefits we get from having a hive, mana-bonding, and Gil's dragon.

    3. Expansion - We've got one hive. Let's make a second! We benefit from quick expansion (if we can get mana), and there are better hive locations available to us, which we should be able to find. Let's find a spot for a more secure redoubt to fall back to (especially if ogres invade)

    4. Join the Elves - Most likely, the elves and us have goals which align. If we could breach the communication boundary, this might be possible. It's unclear how we could actually do this successfully.

    I figure a good plan will incorporate elements of all of the above. How might that look? Division of labor, now that we've got some slivers. Let's search for a better hive location, attempt to talk to the elves, fortify our current position, survey the local area, and hunt for our food. Here's an example plan.

    [X] Preparations

    Settlers:
    Send 2 slivers as scouts to search for a better (more isolated and defensible) home. Specifically, keep an eye out for great, defensible locations for a hive (ideally a cave). Have them take enough food so they wouldn't need to hunt for two months (so they could find a place and build a hive, then hunt). Go away from ogres.

    Elves:
    Send 1 sliver to go talk to the elves with Gil & medallion. Have Gil introduce us as a friend, and try to convey that we should go with the elves as an ambassador (or at least to talk with them). Use that sliver and Regina's learn language action to attempt to learn the Elvish language. Attempt to convey non-hostile nature and desire to protect against ogress. Use what we know of Gil's language to speak to them (so we're clearly making audible sounds). Send a single generic sliver with claws embedded in branches (so our weapons are clearly "not drawn"), and with edible vegetation from hunting (see below). Repeatedly say "Melon" and "Friend" to start if possible. Carve pictures into the wood on our claws of us, Gil, elves fighting ogres together.

    Defenses:
    Encourage Gil to continue building defenses. He's bad at hunting, but great at murder, let's utilize that and give him helpers. Assign 2 slivers and ourselves to help him build defenses, focusing on things which will kill a lot of ogres, and ideally not use up ammunition. Ideas include: More spike pits, cleared trees (for more warning), digging mazes in the hive (so there are choke-points), making mechanical traps in the forest (e.g. gravity assisted angle grabbers or falling rocks), easily collapsible points (so we can manufacture cave-ins in event of invasion), and sheer drops to pits (which we can avoid because of claws, but ogres would have a difficult time avoiding once they fall in). If our sliver to the elves was killed, focus on learning more of Gil's language while we do this.

    Survey:
    We want that +1 hunting bonus from surveying in our area, and basic knowledge on some neighboring land. Send 2 slivers out to survey. While they survey, they should keep an eye open for mana wells, defensible locations, and sustainability caps, for our land and our neighboring land. Use 3 survey actions total (each on a different section of land).

    Hunting:
    Let's not dig into our food storage. Hunt for food ourselves (2d6) and as two actions for 2 slivers and the second action for one of the survey slivers (10d1) cap at 16. If possible and safe, focus on hunting predators rather than prey, so that our hunting increases the sustainability cap long term. If we have extra food (which we should), give it to Gil and suggest he try to preserve it.

    Summary of actions:

    Queen:
    Learn Elvish through generic sliver or English with Gil (if generic sliver is killed)
    Hunt
    Build Defenses

    Generic Sliver actions:
    Scout for better hive location (4 actions, 2 slivers)
    Communicate with Elves (2 actions, 1 sliver)
    Build Hive Defenses (4 actions, 1 sliver)
    Survey local area - hunting cap, mana-well (3 actions, 2 slivers)
    Hunt (5 actions, 2 slivers & survey sliver)

    Gil (suggested):
    Improve defenses
    Negotiate with elves (if they're ok with just the sliver or everything breaks down with them, go back to making defenses)
    Research food preservation

    The benefits of this plan are that they set up a bunch of contingencies for the next month, and we can choose what to follow next based on what happens. If the elves are willing to talk with us, great, we've got allies, and can coordinate making life safe for us. If not, better to know sooner rather than later. We make our hive more defensible, which is great for keeping us alive if Ogres fight us (which they probably will). We begin looking for a secondary hive which we can flee/migrate to if things go south here (or hopefully set up as a better base if we get into an entrenched war), which should hopefully mitigate the ogre threat (keeps retreat a valid option), we find another mana-well to bond to if we want, we don't burn our food supplies (though we don't really make them bigger), and we get a better idea of what's in the area. Likely, next turn we'll want to focus on expanding our sustainability cap and spawning a lot of slivers, or pursuing one of the leads we set up (if it looks relevant).

    Note: if someone has a better idea to do the actions I've proposed here, tag me in a reply and I'll edit them in (if they're good). Also: @eaglejarl is this plan in an ok format and makes legal moves? For future reference, how can I better organize a plan? Do you just want the summary at the bottom?
